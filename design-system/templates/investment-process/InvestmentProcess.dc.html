Investment Philosophy

Our Clients Take the Center Stage

We at LFA — Lugano Financial Advisors SA believe that our clients, and the relationship we hold with them, are the most valuable assets we own. Our aim is to make them feel more like partners rather than clients. For this reason, our investment process is built and continuously improved, with the purpose of bettering the service and experience delivered to them.

Our success is based on the ability to customize our products and services, tailoring them according to our partners' unique goals, concerns, and values. In time, through our proximity, we aim to become a safe harbor in our clients' minds.

Our decision-making process combines quantitative and qualitative analysis. We embrace technological innovation, yet we remain strong supporters of the human touch, which will never disappear from our offering.

We invest our clients' assets by exploiting our access to both external and internal talents, while striking the proper balance between passive investments and active managers. Yet we keep a close eye on costs — because markets can be volatile, but costs and taxes are forever. Both cost- and tax-efficiency play an important role in our process.

Nonetheless, it is the fact that we invest with prudence and care that portrays LFA as a safe harbor in the imagery of our partners. Prudence and care are not a synonym for lower returns; they are the act of managing resources providently and avoiding unnecessary risks.

The "Why" and the "How" sit at the heart of every portfolio we build.

Investment Selection · Equities · Quantitative Model

Proprietary Methodology Explained (1)

The proprietary model is based on extensive literature and has been widely back-tested. First implemented in 2012, it has been continuously updated since — a multi-factor model that can be applied to any universe, apart from financial companies.

It ranks the constituents of the universe on several factors, including but not limited to:

Growth — a scarce resource; its presence limits the risk of value traps.
Quality — optimisation of all factors of production (capital, employees, R&D) plus balance-sheet and P&L measures.
Valuation — an attractive valuation offers a margin of safety and reduces the risk of permanent losses.
Price Performance — the market anticipates micro / macro condition changes.

Investment Selection · Equities · Quantitative Model

Proprietary Methodology Explained (2)

Each factor

  • Carries a pre-defined weight, based on the objective we are trying to achieve.
  • Is composed of several attributes, each also carrying a pre-defined weight.

The final rank

  • Allocated to each stock by weighting the ranks of every attribute and factor by its exposure.
  • Oscillates from 0.0 (best) to 9.0 (worst). Because the model is sector-agnostic, certain sectors may at times hold no high-ranked stocks.
The purpose: select profitable, cash-generating companies, reasonably priced.
