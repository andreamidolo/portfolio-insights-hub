Why these choices
  • Categorical (8). Anchored on LFA Navy and the brand Rose, extended with sober institutional hues — alpine teal, ochre gold, indigo-mauve, slate, burgundy, steel-blue. All kept dark enough for the white hard-coded labels on treemap/sunburst, and spread across lightness so adjacent slices stay separable — including in greyscale.
  • Sequential. A clean mono-tone ramp from a pale blue tint up to LFA Navy — institutional, unambiguous for the correlation heatmap.
  • Positive / Negative. LFA's brand offers no up/down convention, so I assigned alpine teal = positive (gain) and brand Rose = negative (loss) — finance-standard green-up / red-down, but in LFA's register. Rose also doubles as the bullet benchmark marker, where it reads as a neutral reference highlight.
  • Ink / axis / grid. Navy ink for all text, a hairline grey grid (#E0E4EB), and a muted navy-grey axis — quiet, premium, never competing with the data.
  • Type. Cormorant Garamond titles (the LFA voice) over Mulish for every functional label. Mono is declared for brand completeness though the renderer doesn't consume it yet.
  • Logo + export. The clean LFA mark is embedded in the theme (top-right on export); exportPadding: 44 gives the institutional artwork room to breathe.
Assumptions to confirm
Please confirm or correct: (1) the green-up / red-down semantic mapping (some EU houses invert this) — say the word and I'll flip to a navy-up / rose-down or any convention you prefer; (2) the four extended categorical hues (gold, teal, mauve, slate) aren't in LFA's core guidelines — they're my sober derivations; (3) chart titles in Cormorant serif vs. keeping everything in Mulish for maximum legibility at small sizes.
